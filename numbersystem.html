Number Systems

The number system is a way of expressing numbers in a consistent manner. Numbers are categorized into different types depending on their properties.

Types of Numbers

1. Natural Numbers (N)

These are all positive counting numbers starting from 1.

Example: 1, 2, 3, 4, ...

2. Whole Numbers (W)

These include all natural numbers along with 0.

Example: 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

3. Integers (Z)

These are whole numbers including positive, negative numbers and 0.

Example: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...

4. Rational Numbers (Q)

Numbers that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers where the denominator is not zero.

Example: 1/2, -3/4, 5, 0.25

5. Irrational Numbers

Numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction and have non-repeating, non-terminating decimals.

Example: √2, π, e

6. Real Numbers (R)

All rational and irrational numbers together form the real numbers.

Example: -2, 0, 1/3, √5, π
