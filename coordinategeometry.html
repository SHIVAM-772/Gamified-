Coordinate Geometry

Coordinate Geometry is a branch of geometry where points are located on a plane using a pair of numerical coordinates, usually written as (x, y), representing the horizontal and vertical distances from a fixed point called the origin.

Key Concepts

  • Origin: The point (0,0) where the x-axis and y-axis intersect.
  • Abscissa: The x-coordinate of a point.
  • Ordinate: The y-coordinate of a point.

Distance Formula

Distance between two points P(x₁, y₁) and Q(x₂, y₂):

D = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]

Midpoint Formula

Midpoint of line segment joining P(x₁, y₁) and Q(x₂, y₂):

M = ((x₁ + x₂)/2 , (y₁ + y₂)/2)

Examples

  1. Find distance between points (2, 3) and (5, 7).
  2. Find the midpoint of points (1, 4) and (3, 8).

Graphical Representation

Plot points on a 2D plane using the x-axis and y-axis. Connect points to form lines or shapes, and apply formulas to calculate distance, midpoint, or slope.
