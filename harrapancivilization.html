The Story of the First Cities: Harappan Civilization

The Harappan Civilization (also called Indus Valley Civilization) was one of the earliest urban civilizations in the world, flourishing around 2600–1900 BCE in present-day Pakistan and northwest India.

Discovery of Harappan Sites:

  • Excavations at Harappa and Mohenjo-Daro revealed planned cities.
  • Advanced drainage systems and brick-built houses were found.

Town Planning and Architecture:

  • Well-planned streets in grid patterns.
  • Public baths, granaries, and marketplaces.
  • Standardized weights and measures for trade.

Economy and Society:

  • Agriculture based on wheat, barley, and cotton.
  • Trade with Mesopotamia and nearby regions.
  • Artisans, traders, and laborers formed social structure.

Decline:

  • Possibly due to climate change, river shifts, or invasions.
  • Gradual abandonment of cities around 1900 BCE.
