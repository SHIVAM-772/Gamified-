French Revolution

The French Revolution (1789–1799) was a period of radical social and political change in France. It overthrew the monarchy and led to the rise of democracy and modern political ideas.

Main Causes:

  • Social Inequality: Division into Three Estates
  • Economic Crisis: Heavy taxation and poor harvests
  • Enlightenment Ideas: Liberty, Equality, Fraternity
  • Weak Monarchy: King Louis XVI's mismanagement

Key Events:

  • Storming of the Bastille (14 July 1789)
  • Abolition of Feudal Privileges
  • Declaration of the Rights of Man and Citizen
  • Execution of King Louis XVI
  • Rise of Napoleon Bonaparte

Socialism in Europe & Russian Revolution

The 19th and early 20th centuries saw the rise of socialism as a response to the inequalities of industrial Europe. Socialists wanted a fairer society with equality for all.

Socialism in Europe:

  • Reaction to Industrial Revolution and worker exploitation
  • Key thinkers: Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels
  • Goals: End class divisions, promote equality, and public ownership of resources

Russian Revolution (1917):

  • February Revolution: Ended Tsarist autocracy
  • October Revolution: Led by Bolsheviks under Lenin
  • Establishment of Soviet State (USSR)
  • Impact: Inspired socialist movements worldwide
