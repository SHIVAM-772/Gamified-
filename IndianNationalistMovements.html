Indian Nationalist Movement

Early Nationalists

Moderate leaders like Dadabhai Naoroji, Gopal Krishna Gokhale worked within constitutional framework.

Extremists

Leaders like Bal Gangadhar Tilak, Lala Lajpat Rai, Bipin Chandra Pal demanded more radical changes.

Partition of Bengal

1905, the British partitioned Bengal, leading to widespread protests and Swadeshi movement.

Home Rule Movement

Led by Annie Besant and Tilak, it sought self-government within the British Empire.

Gandhian Era

Mahatma Gandhi introduced non-violent resistance, Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, and Quit India movements.

1885 - Indian National Congress Formed

Moderates lead the movement initially.

1905 - Partition of Bengal

Triggered the Swadeshi Movement and protests.

1919 - Jallianwala Bagh Massacre

Galvanized national sentiment against British rule.

1920-22 - Non-Cooperation Movement

Gandhi mobilized masses to boycott British goods and institutions.
