British Economic Policy and Its Effects in India

Land Revenue Systems

Permanent Settlement, Ryotwari, and Mahalwari systems heavily taxed farmers, causing widespread distress.

Deindustrialization

British policies led to decline of traditional handicrafts and local industries, forcing Indians into agriculture.

Drain of Wealth

Revenue and profits were exported to Britain, resulting in India's economic stagnation.

Famines

Economic exploitation and neglect caused recurring famines, leading to massive loss of life.

1765 - British Take Over Revenue Collection

Introduction of Permanent Settlement in Bengal.

1800-1850 - Expansion of British Economic Policies

Spread of Ryotwari and Mahalwari systems, deindustrialization of local crafts.

1857 onwards - Widespread Economic Effects

Famines and increased drain of wealth intensified.
