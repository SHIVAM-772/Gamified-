Quadrilaterals

A Quadrilateral is a polygon with four sides, four vertices, and four angles. The sum of the interior angles of a quadrilateral is always 360°.

Types of Quadrilaterals

  • Parallelogram: Opposite sides are parallel and equal. Opposite angles are equal.
  • Rectangle: Opposite sides are parallel and equal, and each angle is 90°.
  • Square: All sides equal, all angles 90°.
  • Rhombus: All sides equal; opposite angles are equal; diagonals intersect at 90°.
  • Trapezium (Trapezoid): Only one pair of opposite sides is parallel.
  • Kite: Two pairs of adjacent sides are equal; one diagonal bisects the other at right angles.

Important Properties

  • The sum of all interior angles of a quadrilateral is 360°.
  • Diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other.
  • Diagonals of a rectangle are equal and bisect each other.
  • Diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles.
  • Diagonals of a square are equal, bisect each other at 90°.
  • In a kite, one diagonal bisects the other at right angles.

Key Theorems

  1. Parallelogram Diagonal Theorem: The diagonals of a parallelogram bisect each other.
  2. Midpoint Theorem: The line joining the midpoints of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side and half its length (used to prove properties of parallelograms).
  3. Angle Sum Property: The sum of the angles of a quadrilateral is 360°.

Example Problems

  1. The angles of a quadrilateral are in the ratio 3:4:5:6. Find all angles.
    Solution: Sum = 360°, so 3x + 4x + 5x + 6x = 360 → 18x = 360 → x = 20°.
    Angles are 60°, 80°, 100°, 120°.

  2. One angle of a parallelogram is 70°. Find the remaining angles.
    Solution: Opposite angle = 70°, adjacent angles = 180° − 70° = 110°.

Fun Fact

The word quadrilateral comes from the Latin words “quadri” (four) and “latus” (side).
