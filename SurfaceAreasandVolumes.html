Surface Areas and Volumes

Class 9 Mathematics – Odisha Board

Introduction

This chapter deals with measuring the surface area (outer covering) and volume (space occupied) of 3-D shapes like cube, cuboid, cylinder, cone, sphere and hemisphere. These concepts are used in real life for painting walls, filling containers, construction and more.

Important Solids

  • Cube: All sides equal, 6 square faces.
  • Cuboid: Rectangular box with length (l), breadth (b), height (h).
  • Cylinder: Two circular bases connected by a curved surface.
  • Cone: Circular base tapering to a vertex.
  • Sphere: Perfectly round 3-D shape.
  • Hemisphere: Half of a sphere.

Key Formulas

Shape Total Surface Area Volume
Cube (side a) 6a² a³
Cuboid (l, b, h) 2(lb + bh + hl) l × b × h
Cylinder (radius r, height h) 2πr(h + r) πr²h
Cone (radius r, slant height l) πr(l + r) ⅓ πr²h
Sphere (radius r) 4πr² 4/3 πr³
Hemisphere (radius r) 3πr² 2/3 πr³

Example Problems

  1. A cube of side 5 cm. Find its surface area.
    Solution: 6 × 5² = 150 cm².
  2. Find the volume of a sphere of radius 7 cm.
    Solution: 4/3 × π × 7³ ≈ 1437 cm³.
  3. A cylindrical tank has r = 3 m and h = 7 m.
    Volume = π × 3² × 7 ≈ 198 m³.
