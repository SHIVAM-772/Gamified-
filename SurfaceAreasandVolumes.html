Introduction

This chapter deals with measuring the surface area (outer covering) and volume (space occupied) of 3-D shapes like cube, cuboid, cylinder, cone, sphere and hemisphere. These concepts are used in real life for painting walls, filling containers, construction and more.

Important Solids

  • Cube: All sides equal, 6 square faces.
  • Cuboid: Rectangular box with length (l), breadth (b), height (h).
  • Cylinder: Two circular bases connected by a curved surface.
  • Cone: Circular base tapering to a vertex.
  • Sphere: Perfectly round 3-D shape.
  • Hemisphere: Half of a sphere.
