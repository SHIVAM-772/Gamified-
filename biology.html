Class 9 Life Science

The Fundamental Unit of Life

Life is composed of tiny units called cells. Cells are the **basic structural and functional units of life**. All living organisms are made of cells, which carry out essential life processes.

Types of Cells:

  • Prokaryotic Cells: No nucleus, simple structure (e.g., bacteria)
  • Eukaryotic Cells: Contain nucleus and organelles, complex structure (e.g., plants, animals)

Functions of Cells:

  • Metabolism
  • Growth
  • Response to stimuli
  • Reproduction

Tissues

A tissue is a group of similar cells that perform a specific function together. Tissues provide structure, support, and function to the organism.

Animal Tissues:

  • Epithelial Tissue: Covers body surfaces and lines cavities
  • Connective Tissue: Supports and connects body parts (e.g., bones, blood)
  • Muscular Tissue: Enables movement (e.g., skeletal, cardiac, smooth muscles)
  • Nervous Tissue: Transmits signals and controls body activities

Plant Tissues:

  • Meristematic Tissue: Actively dividing cells for growth
  • Permanent Tissue: Differentiated cells for support, storage, and photosynthesis
