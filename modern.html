Pastoralists in the Modern World

Pastoralists are communities that depend on domesticated animals for their livelihood. They move from place to place in search of pastures and water.

Features:

  • Livelihood based on herding animals
  • Nomadic or semi-nomadic lifestyle
  • Trade of animal products like milk, wool, and meat

Impact of Modern Changes:

  • Loss of grazing land due to industrialization
  • Government policies limiting mobility
  • Challenges in maintaining traditional lifestyles

Peasants and Farmers

Peasants and farmers formed the backbone of the agricultural economy. Their lives were shaped by colonial policies and social structures.

Challenges Faced:

  • Heavy taxes imposed by colonial rulers
  • Loss of land and indebtedness
  • Exploitation by landlords and moneylenders

Peasant Movements:

  • Resistance against unfair revenue systems
  • Protests against land dispossession
  • Struggles for better working conditions
