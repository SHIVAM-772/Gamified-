Class 9 English Grammar Units

Adverbs and Adverbials

Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs. Example: She runs quickly.

Adverbials are phrases or clauses that give information about time, place, manner, reason, etc.

Negatives and Interrogatives

Negatives: Words that express the denial or absence of something. Example: I do not like spinach.

Interrogatives: Words used to ask questions. Example: What is your name?

The Predicate Phrase

The predicate tells what the subject does or is. Example: She is reading a book.

It includes the main verb and any auxiliary verbs or modifiers.

Auxiliaries

Helping verbs used with main verbs to form tenses, questions, and negatives. Example: She is studying.

Common auxiliaries: is, am, are, have, has, do, does.

Interrogatives

Words or phrases used to ask questions. Example: Who is at the door?

Common interrogatives: Who, What, Where, When, Why, How.

The Noun Phrase

A group of words that acts as a noun. Example: The tall, young boy is my friend.

Includes determiners, adjectives, and the main noun.

Pre & Post Modifiers in the Noun Phrase

Pre-modifiers: Words before the noun (adjectives, determiners). Example: The beautiful garden

Post-modifiers: Words after the noun (clauses, prepositional phrases). Example: The man who is standing there.
